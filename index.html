Who am I

I'm Marlon Caisip, I've designed applications, and websites that are more simpler and easy to use, lessening the friction to the users. I have spent time learning about design principles and practicing my skills to design.

My Entire Life Story Track Record

I started learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript a few years ago. However, designing for user experience has always been a passion of mine. I graduated from Soledad R. Villafuerte Elementary School, finished my Junior High School at CBSUA - Sipocot, and Senior High School at King Thomas Learning Academy Inc. I am currently taking a bachelor's degree in Information Technology at CBSUA Sipocot.

My interest in programming and designing started when website development was introduced to us by our instructor, Tito Tabs. At that time, AI wasn't yet available, so going to the library, reading books, watching YouTube tutorials, reading guides, and docs were the key to developing my skills.

After my pursuit of basic website development, I stumbled upon powerful programming languages like C#, C++, Java, and so on. However, Python was the one that caught my attention. The majority of the community agrees that it is the most efficient and friendly programming language to learn. So, I studied this language in pursuit of machine learning, to be specific, creating my own Jarvis as I was interested in Iron Man's AI assistant.

Things turned out my way, I was having a hard time learning Python for a few reasons. I stopped for a while and then shifted to VFX effects, where Adobe products were first introduced to me.

Not so long after, I had to take a break and focus on refining my skills as a musician. I studied the art of music, beginner to intermediate piano lessons, and music theory.

After a long while, I got interested in operating systems. So, I went back and forth to using Windows and Linux. Personally, I have fun using Linux, in fact, using Linux made me considered Windows a trash OS. But the majority of my time, I use Windows, and it's great.

It was a long journey for me but I learned so many skills through that, and right now, I'm learning Java, SQL, & Angular. I might study Python soon, and name my project JARVIS. The innovation are massive, so I'm trying to keep up, and this website is the proof of that.

What I do

I make simple yet functional designs that make people's lives easier such as application, website, and product design. Too much visual clutter can be distracting, so I make it direct and honest through user-first design principles that is both visually appealing and easy to understand. Designing is an endless process of trying new approach that is beneficial to users experience, as well as finding out what's pleasing to eyes yet meaningful and useful, and solving problems that matters. User Experience Design will evolve far greater than it is now, and in the latter future the Interface Design, Product Design, and Service Design will change, and its important for developers to be able to catch up with these developments.

I have some knowledge of Java and Python, and I am proficient in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, user experience design, user journey mapping, and UI/UX design.
